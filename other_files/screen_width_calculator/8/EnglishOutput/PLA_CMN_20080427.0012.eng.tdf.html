2008-04-27 06:17:40 Source: PLA Daily, Page 3The Olympic torch relay was held in the morning this day.Early in the morning, more than one hundred thousand local residents and overseas Chinese rushing from various parts of Japan waited for the Olympic torch along the relay route.At 8:25 a.m. local time, the relay event began as the first torch bearer, head coach Senichi Hoshino of the Japanese baseball team, ran out from the relay kick-off ceremony site, holding the torch in his hand.The relay route is about 18.7 kilometers long, and a total of 80 torch bearers participated in the relay.Torch bearers came from all walks of life in society. There were famous athletes such as Kosuke Kitajima, Saori Yoshida, Ai Fukuhara, and Mizuki Noguchi as well as blind people, mentally retarded and severely hearing-impaired individuals, and polio survivors.In addition, among them were also three Chinese torch bearers including Chinese Ambassador to Japan Cui Tiankai.Late April happened to be a time when peach blossoms were in full bloom in Nagano.Along the relay route, people were waving colorful flags and displaying various kinds of slogans to welcome the advent of the Olympic torch.A white-haired Japanese elder, dressed in an overcoat with "Grateful to My Chinese Parents for Bringing Me up" written on it, stood on the side of the street, greeting the relay convoy.In addition some people were holding high the slogans of "Opposing American and European media for distorting facts."A female overseas Chinese student was all tears when she saw the Olympic torch. Waving the five-star red flag in her hand, she was shouting "Go China!"She told reporters that she and some of other classmates took a 12-hour car ride to rush to Nagano overnight. Every one of them was extremely excited when they finally saw the Beijing Olympics torch.
