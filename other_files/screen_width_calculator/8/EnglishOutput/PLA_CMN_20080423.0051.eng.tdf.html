2008-04-23 06:22:32 06:22:32 Source: PLA Daily eighth pageXie Zhenhua revealed this news at the National High-Efficiency Lighting Product Promotion Work Conference which was jointly convened by the Ministry of Finance and the National Development and Reform Commission.In this conference, the Ministry of Finance and the National Development and Reform Commission laid out the first promotion tasks for the 50 million highly efficient lighting products that received financial subsidies.For this, Xie Zhenhua said that vigorously promoting highly efficient lighting products is an important measure in realizing China's "Ten One Five" energy saving and emissions reduction goals.By 2010, China will promote 150 million highly efficient lighting products through the financial subsidy method, which will save a total of 29 billion kilowatt hours, the equivalent of building 6 fewer thermal power plants with installation capacity of 1 million kilowatts.Xie Zhenhua said that vigorously promoting highly efficient lighting products also corresponds to the urgent needs of global climate change.In 2006, an International Energy Agency research report proposed that if, starting now, the whole world gradually eliminated incandescent lights, by 2030, the entire world could save 38% of electricity used for lighting and reduce CO2 emissions by 16.6 billion tons.In February of last year, the Australian government was the first to announce that incandescent lights would be banned by 2010 at the latest, making it the world's first country to plan to ban the use of traditional incandescent lights.
